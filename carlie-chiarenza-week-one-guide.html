Common First-Week Questions

What exactly does "Sr. Manager, Enablement Onboarding" mean at OutSystems?
You own the Revenue Launchpad program — the company's flagship new hire onboarding experience for the field. This means program design, curriculum development, cohort logistics, facilitation, measurement, and continuous improvement. Your mandate is to make every new revenue team member Day One ready, faster and more thoroughly with every cohort. You're the architect and operator of the program. Your closest collaborators are Paul Golding, Carolyn Birsky, and Malorie Weber.
What was Revenue Launchpad's April Lisbon cohort and what did it produce?
Revenue Launchpad is OutSystems' first-ever field-specific onboarding bootcamp, held April 14–16 in Lisbon with ~40 attendees — AEs, SDRs, SAs, and frontline managers. Every graduate left with a fully researched territory POV, a prospecting plan, and an account entry package for a real target account. The cohort was celebrated company-wide. There were competition winners for best personalized email (Jacob Robbins) and best cold call opener (Calvin Faust). The May cohort is your first deliverable.
What is Agentic Systems Engineering and why do I need deep fluency in it?
ASE is OutSystems' new platform category announced March 31, 2026. The company now leads with a platform that governs, secures, orchestrates, and scales enterprise AI. Every Launchpad graduate must be fluent in this narrative — it's what they'll be selling from Day 1. You cannot design great onboarding for a product you don't understand yourself. Read the ASE FAQ and watch the product sizzle video in week 1.
What is the May Launchpad cohort plan and what do I need to do to prepare?
Per Kim Seabrook's announcement in March, the May cohort targets Q1 new hires not in the April cohort, April new hires across all customer-facing roles, and potentially Q3–Q4 2025 hires. Timing and location are TBD — your first action is to get this clarity from Bobbie Jo in your week 1 1:1. Then audit the April curriculum (with Paul and Carolyn), identify the top improvements, and update content to reflect current Sales Plays (BFS, Insurance, Compete vs. Pro-Code).
How does my role differ from Malorie Weber's? We both joined the same week.
You and Malorie are complementary, not overlapping. You own onboarding — the structured program that gets new hires to Day One ready status (Revenue Launchpad, the bootcamp, the curriculum). Malorie owns ongoing field enablement for Americas reps after they've graduated — the continued Sales Play delivery, Agentic Thursdays, coaching cadences. The handoff point between your program and her ongoing work is the boundary you should align on explicitly in week 1. You're natural partners.
What is the onboarding agent technical spec and how does it relate to my work?
There's a technical specification for a Claude-powered "Onboarding Agent" — an AI system that acts as a prescriptive, proactive guide for new sales reps through their entire ramp period. It would run on a daily schedule, track milestones, flag coaching needs to managers, and respond to rep questions. The spec document is in Google Drive. This future-state system is directly adjacent to your program — get the document, understand it, and think about how your Launchpad program design should anticipate and integrate with it.
What Sales Plays do Launchpad participants need to know before they graduate?
In Q2 2026, active plays are: Banking & Financial Services (live since April 8), Insurance (live since April 14), and Compete vs. Pro-Code & AI-Native (live since April 21). A new play launches every Sales Play Tuesday through May. Your Launchpad curriculum needs to ensure every graduate can execute the plays that are live at the time of their cohort. Connect with Bobbie Jo and Sarah Cropp to ensure your curriculum stays synchronized with the rolling play schedule.
What is the ONE Conference and should I attend?
ONE '26 is the company's flagship customer and prospect conference, June 2–3, 2026.
