Common First-Week Questions

   
 
 
   
What is Agentic Systems Engineering and how does it affect what I build?
   
ASE is the platform category OutSystems announced on March 31, 2026 — repositioning from low-code to the platform that governs, orchestrates, and scales enterprise AI agents. As an engineer, this means you're building the infrastructure that powers agent creation, execution, and governance at enterprise scale. Read the ASE FAQ and watch the product sizzle demo to understand what you're building toward.
 
 
   
What's the engineering team's current sprint focus?
   
Ask Sérgio Oliveira for the current sprint board link and the Q2 OKRs on Day 1 — these tell you what the team is optimising for right now. The broader roadmap lives in the Product Roadmap doc. If something isn't in the current sprint, it's either backlogged or deprioritised — don't assume it's being worked on.
 
 
   
What does the engineering onboarding program look like?
   
Engineering onboarding at OutSystems is structured around milestones: first PR merged by end of week 1, first independent ticket shipped by end of week 2, first on-call rotation by end of month 1. Ask Sérgio Oliveira for the full onboarding checklist and timeline. The programme is self-paced but your manager will check in on progress weekly.
 
 
   
How does the performance review and promotion cycle work?
   
Reviews happen Twice a year. The Engineering Career Ladder defines expectations at each level. Ask Sérgio Oliveira how your current level is defined and what 'exceeds expectations' looks like in your first 1:1 — don't wait until review season. Promotions are driven by demonstrated impact, not tenure.
 
 
   
How do I get a PR reviewed? What's the expected turnaround?
   
This is now one of our most common objections. The answer: AI tools create a "hidden stack" of architecture and security debt. Challenge their low-upfront-cost assumption — ask how many specialist engineers it takes to get a prototype to secure production. Use the Competitive Playbook to go deeper. Proof points: Axos Bank (60% faster, 85% less rework), Petrobras (48 solutions in 8 months, 15× ROI).
 
 
   
Where do I get help when I'm stuck on a technical problem?
   
Three paths: (1) ##rd-release-engineering-support — ask your immediate team first. (2) #engineering-ama — the broader engineering org. (3) #ase-internal — for platform-specific questions about Agent Workbench or ASE. Before posting, search Confluence for existing ADRs or runbooks — the answer is often already documented.
 
 
   
What is the ONE Conference and why does Engineering attend?
   
ONE '26 is OutSystems' annual flagship customer conference, June 2–3, 2026. Engineers attend to demo new platform features, support customer technical sessions, and hear directly from the users of what you build. Apply via the ONE '26 Internal App by April 24 (applications closed). Ask Sérgio Oliveira whether your team has dedicated slots and how attendance is decided.
 
 
   
Where do I find the most current technical documentation?
   
Confluence is the source of truth for architecture docs, runbooks, and engineering standards. Never rely on a doc you found in a random Google Drive folder — it may be stale.
