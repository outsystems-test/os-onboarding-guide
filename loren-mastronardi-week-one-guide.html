§ Welcome

You're joining OutSystems at one of the most pivotal moments in our history. In 2026 we set an ambitious V2MOM — grow to €550M ARR, put 10,000 AI agents in production, and reach 100% V2MOM coverage across every employee. The People function is at the centre of that: hiring the talent, developing the skills, and building the culture that makes it possible.

Your first week is structured and purposeful. By Friday, you should have your core systems live, your key relationships started, and a clear picture of your function's Q2 priorities. The People team has invested seriously in making you effective from Day One — lean into it.

Common First-Week Questions

What is Agentic Systems Engineering and why does it matter to the People team?
ASE is OutSystems' new platform category announced March 31, 2026 — repositioning us as the governed AI platform for enterprise agentic systems. As a People professional, you need to understand what we build and sell: it shapes the talent profiles you hire for, the skills you develop, and how you talk to candidates about the company's direction. Watch the product sizzle video and read the ASE FAQ in your first week.
What is the V2MOM and what's my role in it?
The V2MOM (Vision, Values, Methods, Obstacles, Measures) is OutSystems' operating framework — every employee from the CEO down has one. A key Q2 company target is 100% employee V2MOM coverage by end of Q2 2026. The People team directly owns driving and supporting this programme. Log in to the V2MOM App on Day 1, read the company and your manager's V2MOM, and ask Sofia Bué Alves about your team's role in the Q2 completion push.
Who are my key internal clients and how do I engage them?
Your primary client groups are People — Development & Experience. Your key contacts are Kate Guarino and Sofia Bué Alves. Prioritise intro 1:1s with both in your first week. Come prepared with questions about their current headcount challenges, development priorities, and any open People asks. Don't wait for them to come to you.
What People systems do I need access to on Day 1?
Your core stack: Workday (employee records and org chart), Greenhouse (hiring pipelines), Docebo (learning programmes), Lattice (performance and engagement), and the V2MOM App. If any access is missing on Day 1, post in #digital-spot immediately. Don't wait — blocked access in week 1 costs time you can't recover.
What is the current active People programme I'm joining?
The current active programme is Q2 V2MOM completion sprint (goal: 100% by end of Q2 2026). The next key milestone is V2MOM submission deadline — end of Q2 2026. Ask Sofia Bué Alves on Day 1 exactly where things stand and what your immediate contribution should be. Don't assume you have weeks to warm up — there may be live deliverables you're joining mid-flight.
Where do I find People policies, templates, and frameworks?
The People Google Drive is the source of truth: https://drive.google.com. Key documents: Employee Handbook, Job Description Template Library, Career Ladder, Comp Framework, Performance Framework, and Manager Guide. Links are in the Resources section of this guide. Never use a document found outside the official People Drive — it may be outdated.
What is the ONE Conference and is it relevant to me?
ONE '26 is OutSystems' flagship customer and prospect conference, June 2–3, 2026. It's primarily a revenue event but People team attendance is considered on a case-by-case basis — check with Sofia Bué Alves. There's also an active internal registration campaign (the ONE World Cup SPIFF) which you may need to support from a People / headcount reporting angle. Apply via the ONE Internal App if eligible.
How do I stay connected to what's happening across the business?
Three habits to build from Day 1: (1) Read #one-revenue-team daily — this is where business-critical updates land. (2) Check the V2MOM App weekly — it reflects shifting company priorities in real time. (3) Monitor #general for leadership announcements, org changes, and culture moments. The People function is most effective when it's connected to business context, not operating in isolation.
