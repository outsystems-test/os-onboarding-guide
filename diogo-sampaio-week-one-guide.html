Common First-Week Questions

What exactly is the Agentic Enablement Scout and what am I expected to deliver?
A Scout is a 3-week fast experiment sprint. Your mandate: determine whether a Claude-powered assistant connected to multiple sources (Slack, Drive, Seismic, Confluence) can give revenue teams a single, trusted, cited interface to ask questions. You return evidence and a map — what worked and what's concretely possible, what didn't and why, and what a Strike team would need to take it further. Not a polished product. Read the Big Bet Brief on Day 1.
How does the V2MOM app fit with the Scout? Are these separate workstreams?
Yes, they're separate — but you're the Tech Lead for both. The V2MOM app is a live production tool used for company-wide goal alignment. The Scout is a time-boxed experiment. In week 1, the priority is getting up to speed on both. Ask Catarina for the relative time split expectation — and escalate early if the V2MOM app has live issues that conflict with Scout sprint time.
Who are my end users and how do I talk to them?
Your primary end users are customer-facing revenue roles: AEs, SDRs, SAs, CSMs. The best proxy for their needs is #one-revenue-team — read every Revenue Pulse post and notice what questions come up repeatedly. For direct user research, ask Bobbie Jo Frazier or Catarina to connect you with 2–3 reps for short interviews. The Scout brief calls for testing with actual users before week 2 ends.
What is the Agentic Systems Engineering category and why does it matter for my work?
ASE is the new platform category OutSystems launched on March 31, 2026 — repositioning from "low-code app dev" to "the platform that governs, orchestrates, and scales enterprise AI." Your work as an internal product engineer directly enables the field team to understand and sell this narrative. Every rep who misses a Sales Play update because they can't find it is a failure your system is designed to prevent. Watch the product sizzle video and read the ASE FAQ.
What's the difference between what I'm building and NotebookLM?
NotebookLM is used today as a supplementary Q&A tool by field reps — links to NotebookLM notebooks appear weekly in the Revenue Pulse. The Scout is evaluating whether OutSystems can own this capability internally, with richer integrations (live Slack data, real-time Sales Play updates, Seismic), better citations, and a purpose-built UX for the revenue team. Your competitive landscape includes NotebookLM, Slack AI, and Seismic's own search. The brief calls for a structured comparison.
Where do I find the technical architecture decisions for the Scout?
The Enablement Intelligence Platform doc in Drive has a full architecture breakdown across 4 pillars: Knowledge Search (RAG pipeline), Onboarding Agent, Prescriptive Coaching, and Content Management. The recommended build sequence starts with the content + indexing layer. Use this as your technical brief. It was written to help you understand the scope before committing to architecture decisions.
Where do I go when I'm blocked on Slack AI access or pricing data?
The WIP Scout doc flags a pending question to Hugo Catarino about Slack AI availability and renewal cost. Ping him directly in Slack — this is a live dependency for the comparison workstream. If he's unresponsive, escalate to Catarina. The Scout brief explicitly calls for the Slack AI evaluation, so you need this data before week 2.
What does "kill, continue scouting, or convert to Strike team" actually mean at the end?
Kill means the experiments showed the approach doesn't work — wrong technology, wrong problem framing, or better alternatives exist. Continue scouting means early signals are promising but not enough evidence yet — more time needed before committing resources. Convert to Strike team means you've proven the core assumptions hold and the business is ready to commit engineering resources to build the full solution. Your evidence quality determines which path the business chooses — make it falsifiable.
