Common First-Week Questions

What does "GTM Planning & Execution" mean at OutSystems specifically?
Kim Seabrook described your role as "bringing greater alignment across planning, systems, and execution" — and noted your impact "will be felt broadly by the Field teams." Concretely: you design how OutSystems goes to market, ensure the right plays are executed consistently across regions, and that RevOps systems and data infrastructure support the field's ability to close. Start by mapping the current state of Sales Play execution, SPIFF mechanics, and where the field feels friction.
What is Agentic Systems Engineering and why do I need to understand it?
ASE is OutSystems' new platform category announced March 31, 2026. Instead of "low-code app dev," the company now leads with a platform that governs, secures, orchestrates, and scales enterprise AI. All your GTM execution work is in service of making this new positioning land with customers globally. Read the ASE FAQ and watch the product sizzle video in week 1.
What's the Sales Play cadence and how does it work?
Sales Play Tuesday is a weekly enablement session where a new vertical or competitive play is launched to the field. Q2 plays so far: BFS (April 8), Insurance (April 14), Compete vs Pro-Code/AI-Native (April 21). A new play drops every Tuesday through May. The field tags relevant opportunities in Salesforce under the Primary Sales Play dropdown. Understanding how these plays are built, deployed, and measured is core to your GTM mandate — connect with Bobbie Jo Frazier to understand the full process.
What is the SDR team's relationship to RevOps and to my role?
The SDR team recently moved under Revenue Operations — Josh Dixson now reports to Madeline Dibble. This is a direct adjacency to your GTM execution mandate. The SDR motion drives top-of-funnel pipeline, and your planning work should include how SDR execution aligns with the vertical Sales Play cadence, Ada (the AI SDR on outsystems.com), and field execution. Meet Josh Dixson in week 1 to understand pipeline generation mechanics and current priorities.
What is Revenue Launchpad and how does it connect to my work?
Revenue Launchpad is OutSystems' field-specific onboarding bootcamp, held in Lisbon. The April 2026 cohort graduated April 14–16. Graduates leave Day One ready with a territory POV and account entry package. As VP GTM Planning, scaling and improving this program is within your sphere — it directly affects how fast new hires become productive contributors to the GTM plan. Connect with Bobbie Jo Frazier for details and upcoming cohort dates.
What is ONE Conference and what should I know about it?
ONE '26 is the company's flagship customer and prospect conference, June 2–3, 2026. It's a major deal acceleration event and a significant GTM moment — especially important given the ASE category launch. Apply to attend via the ONE Internal App by April 24. There's also a €15,000 "ONE World Cup" SPIFF for teams who bring the most customer registrations.
How do I understand what the field actually needs from GTM planning?
Start with the weekly Revenue Pulse Headlines (posted in #one-revenue-team by Sarah Cropp) — your window into what the field is being asked to execute. Also attend Sales Play Tuesday and Agentic Thursdays in week 1 to hear what the field is being equipped with. Kim Seabrook's posts set the strategic direction. Schedule calls with Americas RVPs (Cranfill, Morrison, Wooderson, Robinson) in week 2 to hear direct feedback on GTM friction points.
Where do I find current data on pipeline and field performance?
Salesforce is the source of truth for pipeline and opportunity data. The Q2 SPIFF Power BI Dashboard tracks incentive performance. Compass has the new Customer Score for account health (Expertise, Adoption, Outcomes — launched April 8). For SPIFF questions or custom data requests, connect with Sónia Parreira at sales.operations.team@outsystems.com.
